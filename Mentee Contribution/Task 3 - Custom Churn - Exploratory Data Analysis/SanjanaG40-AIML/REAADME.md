# HR Attrition Analysis

## Project Overview

This project performs Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on an HR Attrition dataset to identify factors influencing employee turnover and provide actionable business insights.

## Objective

The main objective of this project is to:

* Analyze employee attrition patterns.
* Identify key factors contributing to employee turnover.
* Generate business insights and recommendations.
* Propose feature engineering ideas for future predictive modeling.

## Dataset Information

The dataset contains employee information such as:

* Age
* Department
* Monthly Income
* Job Satisfaction
* Overtime Status
* Years at Company
* Attrition Status

## Data Cleaning

The following preprocessing steps were performed:

* Checked dataset shape and structure
* Identified numerical and categorical columns
* Handled missing values
* Removed duplicate records
* Cleaned categorical data
* Verified data quality

## Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

### Univariate Analysis

* Age Distribution

* Attrition Distribution

### Bivariate Analysis

* Attrition vs Overtime
* Attrition vs Department

### Multivariate Analysis

* Relationship between Age, Income, and Attrition
* Correlation Analysis

## Key Business Insights

1. Employees with lower salaries are more likely to leave.
2. Overtime significantly increases attrition risk.
3. Low job satisfaction is associated with higher employee turnover.
4. Early-career employees exhibit higher attrition rates.
5. Certain departments experience higher attrition than others.

## Feature Engineering Ideas

* Salary Category
* Experience Level
* Overtime Risk Score
* Satisfaction Index
* Promotion Delay Indicator

## Recommendations

* Reduce excessive overtime.
* Improve employee engagement programs.
* Review compensation structures.
* Provide better career growth opportunities.
* Conduct regular employee satisfaction surveys.
